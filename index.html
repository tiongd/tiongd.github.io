Teaching & Educational Philosophy

My Teaching Philosophy

In over a decade of teaching across all levels of the economics curriculum, my approach is grounded in three core principles:

  • Accessibility: Reaching students both inside and outside the classroom with diverse supporting materials.
  • Facilitating Understanding: Emphasizing clear, intuitive explanations that encourage critical thinking rather than memorization.
  • Engagement & Adaptability: Cultivating safe learning environments and tailoring curriculums to meet the specific needs of each cohort.

Factor Models Reading Group

I am facilitating the PhD Reading Group on "The Econometrics of Factor Models" (Semester 1, 2026).

Overview

A 12-week program aimed at graduate students in economics covering foundational factors, classical asset pricing, FAVAR, and Bayesian approaches.

Topics Covered

From Stock & Watson (2002) bridging cross-sectional to time-series, all the way to recent machine learning methods in empirical asset pricing.
